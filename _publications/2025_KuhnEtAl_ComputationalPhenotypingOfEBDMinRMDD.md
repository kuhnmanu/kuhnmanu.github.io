Effort-based decision-making (EBDM) is a crucial mechanism underlying motivation, yet its alterations in remitted major depressive disorder (rMDD) remain unclear. In this study, we examined how individuals with rMDD weigh effort costs and rewards compared to healthy controls. Using computational modeling and behavioral analyses, we found that the rMDD group exhibits reduced reward-driven effort allocation and heightened reward magnitude and probability sensitivit, suggesting persistent motivational deficits despite clinical remission. These findings provide insight into the neurocomputational mechanisms of anhedonia and vulnerability to MDD relapse.
